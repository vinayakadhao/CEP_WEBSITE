Soil Health & Fertility

Healthy soil is the foundation of productive agriculture. It holds nutrients, retains water, and supports plant growth. Maintaining good soil health ensures long-term sustainability and higher yields for farmers.

Understanding Soil Health

Soil health is the continued capacity of soil to function as a vital living ecosystem. This includes providing nutrients, supporting plant and microbial life, filtering water, and storing carbon. Good soil structure promotes aeration and moisture retention, which are essential for crops.

How to Test Your Soil

  • Soil Testing Labs: Collect samples and send them to accredited laboratories for pH, nutrients, and organic matter analysis.
  • DIY Soil Test Kits: Affordable kits are available to check pH, nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium levels at home.
  • Visual Inspection: Observe soil texture, color, water drainage, and root growth.

Improving Soil Fertility

  • Use of compost and organic manure
  • Crop rotation and cover cropping
  • Reduced chemical inputs and pesticide usage
  • Maintaining proper pH balance with lime or gypsum
  • Using biofertilizers and green manures
